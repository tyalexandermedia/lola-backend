# DECISIONS

A running log of the durable product + engineering decisions behind Lola —
the AI Leads Expert. Append-only: when a decision changes, add a new entry
that supersedes the old one (don't rewrite history). Newest last.

> Living document. Coach Ty can paste over any entry with the canonical
> wording. Keep entries short: **what** was decided, **why**, and **what it
> rules out**.

Format per entry: `## <YYYY-MM-DD> — <title>` → Decision / Why / Consequences.

---

## 2026 — Public pricing is two-tier only ($197 / $997)

**Decision.** The public funnel shows exactly two paid tiers: **$197 DIY**
(one-time — "see your score, fix it yourself") and **$997 Full Build**
(one-time, done-for-you + 30 days of Google/AI visibility + GBP + direct
access to Ty, backed by the Half-Back Guarantee).

**Why.** A single, clear step up protects the $997 close. More visible options
dilute the decision.

**Consequences.** No third public price. `/pricing`, header nav, and the
sitemap show only these two.

## 2026 — $297/mo "Lola Managed" is continuity, never a public tier

**Decision.** The monthly is reached only via post-build nurture, the
`/build/start` onboarding pointer, and the `/managed` page. It is deliberately
**off** `/pricing`, the header nav, and the sitemap.

**Why.** Shouting a subscription at cold traffic undercuts the one-time $997
close. Continuity is earned after a delivered win.

**Consequences.** Never SMS the monthly to cold prospects. Managed lives in
email/nurture + the `/managed` page only.

## 2026 — The moat is AI-visibility; keep that engine custom

**Decision.** Showing up in ChatGPT / Perplexity / Gemini answers is the
differentiator. That engine stays built in-house.

**Why.** It's what competitors — and GoHighLevel — can't buy off the shelf.

**Consequences.** We may hand CRM / nurture-editing / per-client ops to GHL at
scale (see below), but the Growth Score + AI-visibility engine stays ours.

## 2026 — "Growth Score", never "audit"

**Decision.** The free top-of-funnel lead magnet is always the **Growth
Score**. The word "audit" is never used in customer-facing copy.

**Why.** "Audit" reads as scary/punitive; "Growth Score" is aspirational and
contractor-friendly.

**Consequences.** Copy reviews reject "free audit" anywhere public.

## 2026 — `/lp/*` landing pages are sacred

**Decision.** `/lp/*` pages are static (generated by `scripts/gen_lp.py` +
`gen_tbpc_*`). Do not convert them to client-rendered, change their URLs, strip
content, or remove JSON-LD schema.

**Why.** They're tuned for AI-crawler indexing and paid-traffic conversion;
churn breaks rankings and tracking.

**Consequences.** LP changes go through the generators, not hand edits.

## 2026 — Automation is safe-by-default (dormant until configured)

**Decision.** All outbound automation (email/SMS nurture, Missed-Call
Text-Back, follow-ups) is dormant until a provider (Resend/Twilio) is
configured, only enrolls leads created **after** deploy, advances on attempt
(no infinite resend loops), and suppresses buyers.

**Why.** Prevents accidental retro-blasts to old contacts and duplicate sends.

**Consequences.** New integrations follow the same rule: no-op cleanly when
their env var is blank.

## 2026 — GoHighLevel is the CRM layer, not the funnel

**Decision.** When a Growth Score is submitted, the lead is bridged into GHL
(contact + "growth-score" tag + pipeline stage) via its inbound webhook. GHL
owns CRM / nurture-editing / per-client ops at scale.

**Why.** Lets Ty operate many clients without hand-rolling CRM, while the
custom site + Growth Score + AI-visibility engine stay in-repo.

**Consequences.** The bridge is safe-by-default (dormant unless
`GHL_INBOUND_WEBHOOK_URL` is set) and never breaks the existing lead flow.

## 2026 — The Stripe webhook fails closed

**Decision.** `POST /stripe/webhook` **rejects** requests when
`STRIPE_WEBHOOK_SECRET` is absent, rather than skipping signature
verification. Verification uses a constant-time HMAC-SHA256 compare.

**Why.** Fail-open fulfillment endpoints can be forged to trigger
access-granting side effects. Fail-closed is the safe default for money paths.

**Consequences.** The webhook is inert until the signing secret is set in
Railway (which is required to go live anyway).

## 2026 — No fabricated proof

**Decision.** Only real clients appear as proof: **Sandbar Soft Wash, Tampa
Bay Power Clean, Travels by Val**. Proof sections auto-hide when empty.

**Why.** Trust is the whole sale; invented stats/logos/reviews are
disqualifying.

**Consequences.** Never invent stats, logos, or reviews. Empty → hidden.

## 2026-07-12 — D-013: Managed price standardized to $297/mo

**Decision.** The optional "Lola Managed" retainer is **$297/month** everywhere:
`docs/PRICING.md` (canonical), `db/pricing.py::RETAINER_PRICE`, the follow-up
nurture default (`FOLLOWUP_MANAGED_PRICE`), GO-LIVE, and the Stripe Managed
subscription link when it's created.

**Why.** Docs and code had drifted ($299 in PRICING.md/db/pricing.py vs $297 in
every operational doc and the live nurture copy). Customers were about to be
quoted a price that disagreed with the source of truth.

**Consequences.** $299 is retired. Any surface quoting the monthly uses $297;
the Stripe subscription link must be created at $297.

## 2026-07-12 — D-014: Unverified proof pulled pending verified 30-day results

**Decision.** All hard-number results claims for Sandbar Soft Wash ("5 keywords
ranked in 3 weeks", "More 5-star reviews", map-pack claims) are removed from
customer-facing copy. The Sandbar case-study page (`/case-studies/sandbar`) and
cold-outreach variants C and E are **held** — page gated behind
`VITE_SHOW_SANDBAR_CASE_STUDY`, variants held via `OUTREACH_ACTIVE_VARIANTS`
(default A,B,D) — until the ranking tracker has real day-0 → day-30 receipts.

**Why.** The measurement doc's ranking tables are blank and Sandbar has 0
Google reviews; publishing results before they're measured violates the
no-fabricated-proof rule on a real client with the family name on it.

**Consequences.** Proof copy references only verifiable facts (15+ years,
20+ cities, the live public dashboard). Claims return only with tracker
receipts, by flipping the two flags above.
